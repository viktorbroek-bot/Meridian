The 3 facials worth booking after 35

Most facials feel nice. Three of them actually change your skin. Written by an aesthetician whose studio only offers two of them.

I do this for a living, and I'll tell you what I tell my friends: most facials feel lovely and change nothing. After 35, only a few are worth your money — and exactly which depends on what your skin is actually doing.

My studio only offers two of the three below. I'm including the one we don't do, and where I'd send you for it.

First, why 35 is the line

Your skin didn't get worse. It got slower.

Around this age three things shift at once, and they're the reason your old routine quietly stopped working. Cell turnover slows — the dull surface layer hangs around longer, so skin looks tired even when you're not. Collagen production drops by roughly 1% a year from your mid-twenties on, and by the late 30s that adds up to visible slack and lines that used to bounce back. And your skin holds less water — the "tight by mid-afternoon" feeling no amount of moisturizer at home seems to fix.

That's the whole problem in one sentence: the work your skin used to do on its own, it now needs a little help with. The three facials below each take on one of those shifts — directly, not cosmetically. Everything else is a nice hour you'll forget by Friday.

HydraFacial

2 The corrective one — for tone and texture

Chemical peel

The one that earns a downtime week — and is worth it when it's the right call.
● SUMMONED ASSET
BEFORE / AFTER — pigmentation & toneSummoned by "what you'll notice." Show a realistic 4-week result, ideally with a day-3 "slightly red" shot too — honesty about downtime.
What it actually does.

Removes the top layers of dull, uneven, sun-marked skin so newer skin underneath can surface. Different acids do different jobs — glycolic for texture, mandelic for sensitive skin, TCA for pigmentation. Matched to your face, not pulled off a menu.

What you'll notice.

A few days of slight redness, then flaking around day four to six. By day seven to ten: more even tone, smoother texture, and that "what's different about you" reaction nobody can quite place.

$200–$450depending on depth [verify]. Most see real change after a single well-chosen peel — not the package of six some clinics quote.
Who it's for — and who it isn't +
For: women who've watched their tone shift — sun spots, old breakout marks that won't fade, fine lines that deepen in certain light.
Not for: the week before a wedding. Peels need timing and breathing room — and a clinic that picks the right strength. Too light does nothing; too strong on skin that wasn't ready can set you back.
The honest downside

There's a real downtime window. We've had women panic at day-three redness — which is exactly why we walk you through every day before you book. This is the one treatment where the wrong choice can cost you, so it's the one we're most careful about saying "not yet" to.

Why I'm telling you about it anyway.

Why we don't do it
